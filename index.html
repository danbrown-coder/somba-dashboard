Growth strategy

How SOMBA grows as a whole — and what each platform's job is.

The numbers point to one playbook: Instagram is the anchor (two-thirds of the audience), TikTok is the reach engine (13.8K likes from 151 followers — the content already travels far beyond the follower base), YouTube has proven demand (421K lifetime views, ~1,700 views for every subscriber), and LinkedIn is wide-open ground for a school of management. The job now is converting reach into followers — and doing it without tripling the workload.

The core workflow
Film once → post three times: TikTok → Instagram Reel → YouTube Short

Every vertical video SOMBA already makes for TikTok is also a Reel and a Short. One filming session feeds three platforms — reach triples, effort doesn't.

Instagram

The anchor — deepen it

756 followers and 512 posts make this the established home base. Growth here comes from borrowed audiences and showing up consistently in Reels.

  • 3 Reels a week — reuse the TikTok edits as-is; Reels is where Instagram hands out free reach.
  • Collab posts with Cal Lutheran's main account, student orgs, and featured students — a collab post lands in both audiences' feeds and is the fastest borrow of an existing following.
  • Stories on event days — polls, countdowns, and "tap to RSVP" stickers keep SOMBA at the top of the app for followers who miss posts.
  • Handle everywhere — @somambassadors and a QR code on every flyer, slide deck, and event check-in table.

TikTok

The reach engine — convert it

91 likes per follower means the For You feed already loves this content — strangers watch it, they just don't follow yet. The gap between 13.8K likes and 151 followers is the single biggest free win on this dashboard.

  • End every video with a reason to follow — "follow for CLU business life" beats a generic outro; viewers follow accounts that promise a series, not single videos.
  • Make it a series — "Day in the life of a SOM major," "Internship diaries," "Professor hot takes." Series turn one-time viewers into followers waiting for the next episode.
  • Reply-to-comment videos — TikTok boosts them, and they signal an account worth following.
  • Ride trending sounds within 48 hours — the current library of 440 videos shows the volume is there; timing is the lever.

YouTube

The search asset — make it findable

421K views against 243 subscribers means people arrive through Shorts and search, watch, and leave. The channel earns attention; it needs to keep it.

  • 3 Shorts a week from the TikTok pipeline — zero extra filming.
  • Searchable titles, not hashtag strings — "Cal Lutheran business school day in the life" gets found for years; "#fyp #viral" gets found by no one.
  • Playlists per series — binge-watching a playlist is what converts a viewer into a subscriber.
  • Pinned comment on every Short linking the Instagram — send YouTube's drive-by traffic to the anchor platform.

LinkedIn

The whitespace — plant the flag

3 followers at a school of management is the biggest mismatch on this page — and the easiest to fix, because the ambassadors themselves are the distribution.

  • Every ambassador lists SOMBA as experience on their profile and follows the page — that alone puts the page in front of hundreds of connections.
  • One professional post a week — event recaps, internship wins, alumni spotlights. LinkedIn rewards consistency over volume.
  • Use the page's invite credits — LinkedIn gives company pages free connection invites every month; aim them at SOM students and alumni.
  • Tag the School of Management and Cal Lutheran pages so their followers see SOMBA in the conversation.

Facebook

Optional — decide deliberately

Students aren't there, but parents and alumni are. Worth launching only if those audiences matter to the program's goals.

  • If launched, automate it — Meta Business Suite can cross-post every Instagram post to Facebook automatically, so it costs zero extra effort.
  • Until then, skip it — a dead page looks worse than no page.
